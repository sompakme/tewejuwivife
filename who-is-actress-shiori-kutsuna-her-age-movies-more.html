Biography Timeline

Shiori Kutsuna is a Japanese-Australian actress known for working in Japanese and Hollywood films. She was born to a Japanese mother and an Australian father on December 22, 1992, in Killarney Heights, Sydney, Australia. She started her acting career in Japan and later gained international recognition for her roles in Hollywood productions. Here is a detailed biography timeline of Shiori Kutsuna:

  • 1992: Shiori Kutsuna was born to a Japanese mother and an Australian father in Sydney, Australia.
  • 2006: She moved to Japan with her family and attended acting school in Tokyo.
  • 2010: Kutsuna acted in the Japanese television drama “Kaito Royale.”
  • 2011: She starred in the Japanese film “Beck,” based on the manga series of the same name.
  • 2012: Kutsuna supported the Japanese film “Hara-Kiri: Death of a Samurai,” directed by Takashi Miike.
  • 2013: She appeared in the Japanese drama series “Hanzawa Naoki” and the film “The Backwater.”
  • 2014: Kutsuna made her Hollywood debut in the film “47 Ronin,” alongside Keanu Reeves.
  • 2016: She played a supporting role in the Marvel Cinematic Universe film “Deadpool 2.”
  • 2017: Kutsuna appeared in the Japanese film “The House Where The Mermaid Sleeps” and the American movie “Oh Lucy!”
  • 2018: She played a lead role in the Japanese drama series “Anone.”
  • 2019: Kutsuna appeared in the Japanese film “Makuko” and the American film “The Outsider.”
  • 2020: She played a supporting role in the Japanese drama series “Love Will Begin When Money End.”
  • 2021: Kutsuna starred in the American science-fiction film “Bullet Train,” directed by David Leitch and co-starring Brad Pitt.

Shiori Kutsuna has won several awards for acting, including the Best Supporting Actress award at the 37th Japan Academy Prize for her performance in “Beck.” She has also been named one of Forbes Asia’s 30 under 30 in the entertainment category.
