Ryan Gosling Breaking Up A Fight In NYC IS Another Example As To Why He's One Of The Nicest Guys In

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, August 20, 2024

Highlights

  • Ryan Gosling regretted breaking up a street fight and felt embarrassed about it, considering the context.
  • Fans praised Gosling for getting involved and admired his humility in the situation.
  • Gosling isn't a fan of violence in films, as revealed during his role in "Only God Forgives."

Not only is Ryan Gosling loved for his acting talent, but the man is also recognized for his tremendous personality, which has been on full display during interviews (remember the time an interviewer tried to trick the actor into spoiling one of his films?), and apparently, street fights... In the following, we're going to take a look back at Gosling's NYC encounter with two men, and it was all caught on video.

Gosling was praised for stopping the fight but as it turns out, Ryan regretted the situation given the context of what was taking place. We'll reveal how it all went down, and what the fans had to say about the ordeal looking back.

Fans Praised Ryan Gosling For Getting Involved And Breaking Up A Fight

What makes this viral moment that much better is the fact that the person filming did not realize Ryan Gosling was breaking up the fight... until the very end when she's told "that's the guy from The Notebook." The fight starts with just the two "dudes," and then later, Gosling shows up in a tank top to save the day. Now Gosling did comment on the moment, stating that he regretted it.

However, fans had nothing but praise for the actor stepping in. It was yet another moment that showed how humble the man really is. He could've just walked away and kept his identity a secret, but instead, he decided to get involved.

Fans can relive the moment below.

With nearly 3 million views on the original YouTube clip, fans had a lot to say.

The top comments read, "He can sing, he can act, and he can break up street fights."

"That guy has earned my respect. He didn't have to stop it, and most of the times, celebrities feel like they're in some parallel world above us normal people, but the guy came and helped the situation, good one."

"He pulled them apart like a dad pulling apart his 2 kids fighting lmao. He even has that posture when talking to them, slightly hunched like "now you guys shake hands and apologize."

A great moment for fans, but looking back, Gosling had some regrets about getting involved given the context of the situation.

Ryan Gosling Surprisingly Regretted Breaking Up The Street Fight

Looking back, Ryan Gosling was 'embarrassed' about breaking up the street fight. Gosling gave a little more context to the situation, and it was said that an artist had his painting stolen by a person that had been admiring it for quite some time.

Gosling states that he should've just stayed out of it and let them deal with it on their own. He hilariously said that "the guy from The Notebook made it weirder."

Gosling said about the incident, "I'm embarrassed. I should have just kept my nose out of it. the guy was a fan, and so he wanted the painting so bad he had to steal it because he couldn't afford it."

"So he finally steals the painting, and he's getting his ass kicked by his hero, and then the guy from 'The Notebook' shows up and makes it weirder. The whole thing, nobody wins. Nobody won."

Although Gosling was the hero in the eyes of fans, in the context of the true story, Ryan viewed himself as a villain of sorts... In the end, the actor was just doing his best, and had the right intentions. That's more than enough for fans.

Ryan Gosling Isn't Into Violence In Films Despite His History In Hollywood

Ryan Gosling spoke about violence in films during his role in Only God Forgives. It was a different character for Gosling, and the actor admitted that violence in movies isn't really something he grew up idolizing or watching religiously.

"It's not as much a part of the fabric of my creativity as it is for Nic. I mean, he was watching 'Texas Chainsaw Massacre' when he was a kid before school, so it's just a part of him."

"For me it's been a completely new way of working. It's a new kind of film language that I'm not really adept at."

Gosling's role in the film was a misfire, and it received loud boos at Cannes. Though thankfully, Gosling wasn't there to hear the reaction.

Gosling revealed, "It’s like the worst feeling, because you work on something as intimately as we did on this and to not be able to be there with it is an awful feeling. But also I was directing a film at the time, so it was like “Sophie’s Choice,” you know? Because you’d have to leave halfway through the shoot in the middle of a shoot week to go to Cannes. It was like a “Sophie’s Choice” situation."

We can safely say that Gosling made the right choice...
